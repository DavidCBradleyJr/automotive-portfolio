There is something about a Cars & Coffee meet that never gets old. You roll up before sunrise, coffee in hand, and the lot is already humming with flat-sixes and twin-turbos warming up. Last weekend's March meet was one of the best we have had all year -- perfect cloud cover for shooting, a killer turnout, and more JDM metal than I could keep up with.

The star of the morning was easily this R34 Skyline GT-R that showed up on Midnight Purple III paint. The owner pulled in right as the fog was starting to lift, and the light was doing something magical on those fender flares. I burned through about two hundred frames in ten minutes.

![R34 Skyline GT-R at sunrise with fog](https://res.cloudinary.com/dl0atmtb7/image/upload/w_1200,f_auto,q_auto/v1/gallery/jdm/r34-skyline-sunrise)

Further down the row, a pair of Mk4 Supras were parked nose-to-nose -- one bone stock in white, the other fully built with a single turbo kit poking through the hood. The contrast made for a great side-by-side composition. I shot low and wide to get both front ends in frame with the morning sky behind them.

![Twin Mk4 Supras parked nose-to-nose](https://res.cloudinary.com/dl0atmtb7/image/upload/w_1200,f_auto,q_auto/v1/gallery/jdm/twin-supras-meet)

As the crowd thinned out around noon, I grabbed a few detail shots of an NSX-R that had been hiding in the back corner. The owner had just finished a full Type-R restoration -- new seals, fresh paint in Championship White, and period-correct Mugen components throughout. The kind of car that makes you stop and appreciate the craft.

![Honda NSX-R detail shot of front end](https://res.cloudinary.com/dl0atmtb7/image/upload/w_1200,f_auto,q_auto/v1/gallery/jdm/nsx-r-detail-front)

Already looking forward to the April meet. If the forecast holds, we might get one of those golden-hour evening sessions that are perfect for rolling shots on the way out. Stay tuned.
