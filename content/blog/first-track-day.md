The season opener at the track is always chaos in the best way. Teams are shaking off the winter rust, drivers are relearning their braking points, and the whole paddock has this electric energy that you only get on opening day. I spent most of the morning posted up at turn three where the late-brakers were putting on a show.

Shooting motorsport is a completely different discipline from static car photography. You are dealing with subjects moving at triple-digit speeds, unpredictable lines, and about a half-second window to nail the composition. But when you freeze that perfect moment -- a car fully rotated mid-drift with tire smoke trailing behind -- there is nothing like it.
